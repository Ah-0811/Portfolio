About Cairo

Cairo is a vibrant city full of history and culture, home to the Egyptian Museum, bustling markets, and the majestic Nile River.

Visit the Great Pyramids at Giza, ride along the Nile, and explore ancient temples, mosques, and traditional neighborhoods.

Must-See Attractions

The Egyptian Museum: Houses a world-class collection of ancient Egyptian artifacts, including treasures from Tutankhamun’s tomb.

Khan El Khalili Bazaar: A lively market where you can buy souvenirs, spices, jewelry, and experience traditional Egyptian culture.

Cairo Citadel: A historic fortress with panoramic views of the city and beautiful Islamic architecture.

Al-Azhar Mosque: One of the oldest mosques in Cairo and a center of Islamic learning.

Food and Cuisine

Enjoy authentic Egyptian dishes such as koshari, falafel, and molokhia. Don’t miss trying street food at local markets for a true taste of Cairo.

Travel Tips

Wear comfortable shoes for exploring busy streets, carry bottled water, and plan visits early in the day to avoid the heat. Public transport and taxis are widely available, but traffic can be heavy.
